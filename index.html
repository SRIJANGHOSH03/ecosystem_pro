About Ecosystem Conservation

Ecosystem conservation is crucial for maintaining the balance of our planet's natural systems. By protecting ecosystems, we ensure the survival of various species and maintain the services they provide to humanity.

Conserving biodiversity is essential for sustaining life on Earth. It provides us with food, clean water, medicines, and regulates our climate. Adopting sustainable practices helps us minimize our impact on the environment while meeting our needs.

Red List of Threatened Species

The Red List of Threatened Species, maintained by the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN), identifies species facing the highest risk of extinction. It categorizes species into various threat levels, ranging from Least Concern to Critically Endangered.

As of the latest update, there are over 35,000 species assessed on the Red List, with a significant portion being classified as threatened. Monitoring and addressing the factors driving species decline are crucial for their survival.
